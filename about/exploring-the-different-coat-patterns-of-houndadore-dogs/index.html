The Houndadore is a unique and beautiful breed of dog that combines the traits of both the Labrador Retriever and the Bloodhound. The piebald coat pattern is characterized by large patches of white on a base color. These patches can vary in size and shape, creating a unique and eye-catching appearance. The base color can be black, liver, or any other color commonly found in Labrador Retrievers or Bloodhounds. The white patches are usually evenly distributed throughout the body, but they can also be more concentrated in certain areas, such as the chest or face.

One of the reasons why the piebald coat pattern is so fascinating is its genetic basis. It is caused by a specific gene mutation that affects the distribution of pigment in the hair follicles. This mutation prevents the production of melanin, which is responsible for the coloration of the coat. As a result, the areas where melanin is absent appear white, while the areas where melanin is present retain their base color.
